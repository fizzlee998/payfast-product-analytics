Key findings and recommendations
Finding 01
Retention declining cohort-over-cohort
January cohort retained 43.2% in M+1. June cohort only 27.9% — a 35% deterioration in 6 months. No cohort has stabilised.
Audit acquisition channels. Pause high-churn Paid Ads (95.2% churn).
Finding 02
KYC blocks 82% from ever transacting
Only 4.7% of acquired users make a first transaction. Fintech benchmark is 8–15%. KYC friction at document submission is the primary barrier.
Redesign KYC — step-by-step, save-and-return, live document feedback.
Finding 03
288 At-Risk users = ₦65M recoverable
These users averaged 12.1 transactions and ₦226k prior spend. A personalised re-engagement campaign can recover ~₦26M this month at zero acquisition cost.
Launch WhatsApp campaign with ₦500 cashback offer this week.
Finding 04
Ship Variant B onboarding now
+11% activation lift, p < 0.0001. Every month of delay costs ~₦48M in lost revenue from users who would have activated but did not.
Deploy Variant B to 100% of users within 7 days.
Finding 05
Revenue peaked July, -38% since
Revenue per active user (₦91k–₦103k/month) is excellent. The entire decline is explained by cohort quality deterioration — not user spending less.
5-point retention improvement = ₦30–40M/month recovered by Q1 2025.
Finding 06
141 Power Users drive 41.7% of revenue
Power Users average 27 transactions and ₦545k spend. Premium users spend 6.2x more than Free. Converting 200 Free users to Premium = +₦41M.
Build Power User loyalty programme — priority support, higher limits.
